# Example Workflows to Test

## 1. Customer Support Ticket Resolution

**Team:** Customer Operations  
**Tools:** Zendesk, Slack, Salesforce

**Description:**
Our support team receives 100+ customer tickets daily. Each ticket requires manual categorization (billing, technical, account), searching through multiple knowledge bases, drafting responses, supervisor review, and updating multiple systems. The categorization and research takes 5-10 minutes per ticket, response drafting takes 10-15 minutes. We have inconsistent response quality across agents and 24+ hour response times during peak hours. This runs 100 times daily and involves sensitive customer data requiring privacy compliance.

## 2. Sales Pipeline Management

**Team:** Sales Operations  
**Tools:** HubSpot, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Gmail, Calendly

**Description:**
Sales reps receive 30-40 inbound leads daily from our website form. Process: Rep manually reviews lead info, searches LinkedIn for company details, googles recent news about the company, decides if qualified based on company size/industry, drafts personalized outreach email, sends multiple follow-ups, manually coordinates demo scheduling through 5-10 email exchanges, updates HubSpot with all details. Lead qualification takes 15-20 minutes per lead. Email personalization and follow-ups take another 30 minutes per lead. Demo scheduling is extremely time-consuming. We lose deals due to slow response times.

## 3. Weekly Performance Reporting

**Team:** Operations Management  
**Tools:** Google Sheets, Salesforce, Zendesk, Looker

**Description:**
Every Friday, team leads manually compile weekly performance reports. They log into Salesforce to pull revenue data, Zendesk for support metrics, and custom dashboards for operational KPIs. They copy data into Excel, create 5-6 charts, calculate week-over-week changes, write 3-4 paragraph executive summary highlighting wins and concerns, and email to 15 stakeholders. This takes 3-4 hours every Friday. Data is often copy-pasted incorrectly. Executive summaries vary wildly in quality and focus depending on who writes them. Stakeholders want reports by 10am Friday but often receive them at 3pm.

## 4. Invoice Processing and Approval

**Team:** Finance Operations  
**Tools:** Email, QuickBooks, Slack, Google Drive

**Description:**
Finance team receives 200+ vendor invoices monthly via email. Process: Team member downloads PDF invoice from email, manually extracts vendor name, amount, invoice number, due date into spreadsheet, checks if invoice matches purchase order, if amount is over $1000 requires manager approval via Slack, enters invoice into QuickBooks, files PDF in correct Google Drive folder by vendor and month, marks email as processed. Takes 10-15 minutes per invoice. Frequent data entry errors. Invoices sometimes miss approval deadlines causing late payment fees. Purchase order matching is tedious and error-prone. This is highly repetitive and frustrating work.

## 5. Compliance Document Review

**Team:** Legal & Compliance  
**Tools:** SharePoint, Word, Email

**Description:**
Legal team reviews 20-30 customer contracts monthly for compliance issues. Each contract is 15-40 pages. Lawyer reads entire contract, manually highlights sections with compliance concerns (data privacy, liability, payment terms, termination clauses), searches internal compliance database for company policies, redlines problematic sections with suggested edits, writes 1-2 page summary memo of key risks, sends to business owner for review. Takes 2-4 hours per contract. Very tedious work. Risk of missing critical compliance issues due to fatigue. Inconsistent review quality. High-stakes as compliance failures can result in regulatory fines or legal liability.

## 6. Employee Onboarding

**Team:** People Operations  
**Tools:** BambooHR, Gmail, Slack, Notion

**Description:**
HR coordinator onboards 5-10 new employees monthly. Process: Create accounts in 8 different systems (email, Slack, payroll, benefits, etc.), manually populate employee info in each system, send 5 different welcome emails with links and instructions, schedule 1:1 meetings with manager and team members, add to relevant Slack channels and email lists, ship laptop and equipment, create personalized onboarding checklist in Notion, send reminders throughout first week. Takes 3-4 hours per employee. Extremely repetitive. Coordinators often forget steps or send info to wrong people. New employees get inconsistent experience and sometimes missing access.

---

## 7. Social Media Content Moderation

**Team:** Trust & Safety  
**Tools:** Internal moderation tool, Jira

**Description:**
Moderation team reviews 1000+ flagged posts daily for policy violations. Process: Moderator opens flagged post, reads content and context, checks against 50-page policy document, looks for similar past decisions in Jira, makes allow/remove/escalate decision, if escalate then writes detailed explanation for senior moderator, updates moderation tool with decision, logs decision in Jira with reasoning, moves to next post. Takes 2-5 minutes per post. Extremely repetitive and mentally draining. High inconsistency in decisions between moderators. False positives frustrate users. False negatives create legal/PR risk. Senior moderators are bottlenecked with escalations.
